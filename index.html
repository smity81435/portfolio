Talus Analytics Company Page

In 2018, I developed the company website for Talus Analytics, a small, woman-owned analytics and web development company based out of Washington D.C., with a satellite facility in Boulder, CO.

Talus Analytics deals almost exclusively with organizations that intend to make change on an international scale. With clients like FEMA, the US CDC, NATO, and Georgetown University, Talus is quickly growing past their peers in the field of datavisualization and statistical analysis. While working for them as a UIUX Design and Web Development Inter over the summer of 2018, they requested that I rebuild the company website.

The rebuild project, which had initially started as a simple landing page, quickly became increasingly complex, and before long the company decided to incorporate more and more of their content. The tricky part, was creating a page that the company could update dynamically as they they published more of their work.

To tackle the project, I used a powerful JavaScript library that Talus was using for anumber of their other Data Visualization projects; D3.js. While the D3.js library is normaly used for complex (and stunning) data visualization, I ended up utilizing it to import large datasets full of project descriptions and team information. You can see the result of that implementation in the gif below:

I also assisted developers and data scientists at Talus with the development of a web application being design for the United States Department of Homeland Security named MERIT. MERIT is designed to help community planners to make responsible decisions about their comminuty in regards to disaster mitigation: specifically with flooding. The application requested location data, then presented the user with a detailed analysis of that location, and provided them possible solutions to resiliency problems in the area.

The Application relied heavily on D3.js, and for the first time I was able to witness how the back-end developers could patch in custom API's in order to provide powerful results. Unfortunately, I am not allowed to disclose photos or links to MERIT, or any of the other projects from Talus Analytics ( with the company website being the exception ).

CU Student Veterans Association

The ongoing Graphic Design and Re-Branding of the University of Colorado's Student Veterans Association.

For the past four years, I have been the graphic designer of the Student Veterans Association here at the University of Colorado. I had originally been approached by the President of the SVA to produce t-shirts for the Association, and quickly expanded to making all sorts of products. I have designed, t-shirts, stickers, banners, coozies, flyers, and repeatedly re-designed the logo for them.

In the fall of 2017, I led a group of five students in the "Student Veterans Association Development Project", where we put together a collection of products for them to place in their new office, and gave a fresh new set of products to take into the upcoming year. We also wrote an extensive report on the SVA, and how we thought that it could better expand by using updated advertisements around campus targeting new student veterans.

At some point, I became disenfranchized with going through the campus approval process for all of the new gear that I was producing. They kept telling me that veterans would be targeted on campus if I produced gear for them that other people would see. I completely disagreed, and realized that I needed to take matters into my own hands. Through Teespring.com, I made a new line of gear, that donated a portion of all of the profits to the Wounded Warriors Foundation.

PHAROS Mobile Application Design

The UI/UX and Design of the fictional app Pharos, the world's first Artificially Intelligent Audiobook App.

During a digital design course at the University of Colorado Boulder, I made the UI/UX design and branding for a fictional audiobook app names Pharos. The application, which would utilize artificial intelligence to give you getter book reccomendations, was birthed from my frustrations in finding interesting books to read. The subsequent design practice helped me refine my ability to mold cohesive user experiences.

During the design process of Pharos, utilized a number of applications to achieve the desired result, including:


  • Affinity Designer
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe XD
  • InVision App
  • Sketch

The final projet would include a full 12-page design guide that detailed everything from typography, to color palette, to multiple concepts for an online landing page. This project helped me solidify certain aspects of my design style, and helped me bring out the more refined aspect of my work.
